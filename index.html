Your Execution Plans

Two Paths to the Same Result

Plan A is how you run the system manually with free AI tools today. Plan B is the same system fully automated — also with free tools where possible.

01

Building the Lead List

Input Layer — Raw Volume

Your goal is to pull a massive, unfiltered list of international companies — up to 2,00,000 rows. The bigger and dirtier the list, the better, because AI filters it in Step 2. Don't over-curate here; just export in bulk.

  • Go to Apollo.io (free tier: 50 exports/month) and use filters like country, employee count, industry, tech stack. Export CSVs manually in batches.
  • Use Hunter.io free plan to find verified email addresses for each company domain.
  • Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator free trial or LinkedIn search with Boolean operators to find decision-makers. Save profiles manually.
  • Use Phantombuster free tier (10 min/day) to scrape LinkedIn search results into a spreadsheet automatically.
  • Combine all CSVs into one master Google Sheet. Deduplicate using a free formula or the "Remove Duplicates" add-on.
Free Tools for This Step
Apollo.io (Free) Hunter.io (Free) Phantombuster (Free Tier) Google Sheets LinkedIn Search
All tools above have usable free tiers. No credit card required for initial scraping.
02

Filtering with AI

ICP Qualification — 2L → 40K

Before enriching or emailing anyone, you need to verify each company actually fits your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP). Manually paste batches of lead data into Claude and prompt it to score and filter. This saves you from wasting time enriching bad leads.

  • Open Claude.ai (free) and write a system prompt defining your ICP: industry, company size, region, revenue, job titles, and pain points.
  • Paste 50–100 rows at a time from your Google Sheet and ask Claude to return only the rows that match your ICP with a fit score (1–10).
  • Ask Claude to also flag the reason for fit or disqualification in a "Notes" column — this helps you learn and refine the ICP over time.
  • Keep only leads scored 7+ from Claude's output. Paste them into a new "Qualified Leads" sheet.
  • Repeat in batches until your 2L raw list is reduced to roughly 40K qualified prospects.
Free Tools for This Step
Claude.ai (Free) ChatGPT (Free) Google Sheets Gemini (Free)
Copy-paste workflow — no API costs. Batch 50–100 rows at a time for best results.
03

Let AI Write the Campaign

Email Copywriting + Self-Grading

Use Claude or ChatGPT to generate a full 3-email cold outreach sequence: Email 1 (cold intro), Follow-Up 1 (value add), Follow-Up 2 (soft close). Also generate spintax variations for each subject line and opening line to improve deliverability.

  • Write a prompt for Claude: "Write a 3-email cold outreach sequence for [your service] targeting [ICP segment]. Include spintax variations using curly braces for subject lines. Keep Email 1 under 80 words."
  • Ask Claude to self-grade the copy: "Now score this sequence on: personalization (1–10), clarity of value prop (1–10), and call-to-action strength (1–10). If any score is below 7, rewrite that section."
  • Once graded 7+, copy the sequences into a Google Doc or Notion page labelled by campaign segment.
  • Generate at least 3 angle variations per campaign — different hooks, different pain points — so you can A/B test across SmartLead.
  • Use MailMeteor or Woodpecker free tiers to test subject line open rates before full launch.
Free Tools for This Step
Claude.ai (Free) ChatGPT Free Google Docs Notion (Free) MailMeteor (Free)
Self-grading loop is key — don't skip it. Claude rewrites until the copy scores 7+/10 on your rubric.
04

Human Review Checkpoint

The Only Manual Gate in the System

Before any campaign goes live, you personally review the copy, the angle, and the segment. This is where your judgment protects sender reputation and keeps messaging on-brand. In the manual version, you review in Google Docs or Notion directly.

  • Create a "Campaigns to Review" folder in Google Drive or Notion. AI-written campaigns land here after Step 3.
  • Review three things: (1) Is the angle right for this segment? (2) Is the first line compelling? (3) Does the CTA make a small ask?
  • Leave a comment: "APPROVED — launch as-is" or "CHANGE: [your note]". If change, paste the note back to Claude and have it rewrite.
  • Once approved, move the campaign doc to "Ready to Launch" folder and proceed to Step 5.
Free Tools for This Step
Google Drive Notion (Free) Claude.ai (Rewrite)
This is intentionally manual — your judgment here directly protects sender reputation and deal quality.
05

Launch in SmartLead / Instantly

Manual Campaign Setup in Sending Platform

Once you have approved campaign copy and a verified lead list, you manually set up the campaign inside SmartLead or Instantly (both have free trials). Upload 5,000 leads, configure follow-up timing, and hit start.

  • Use SmartLead.ai free trial or Instantly.ai free trial (both offer 14-day free). Create one campaign per segment.
  • Upload your 5,000 leads as a CSV. Map the fields: first name, company, email, any personalization variables you used in copy.
  • Configure sending schedule: 9am–5pm local time for the prospect's timezone. Limit 40 emails/day per inbox to stay safe.
  • Set follow-up delays: Email 1 → Day 1, Follow-Up 1 → Day 3, Follow-Up 2 → Day 7. Enable auto-stop on reply.
  • For email warm-up (critical for deliverability), use Mailwarm or SmartLead's built-in warm-up — both have free tiers.
Free Tools for This Step
SmartLead (Free Trial) Instantly.ai (Free Trial) Mailwarm (Free) Lemlist (Free Trial)
Run warm-up for 2 weeks on every inbox before sending cold emails. This is non-negotiable for deliverability.
06

Nightly Campaign Audit

Kill Bad Campaigns, Feed Winners

Every evening, you manually check every active campaign in SmartLead and make a decision based on four kill criteria. Bad campaigns stop early to protect sender reputation. Good campaigns get more leads uploaded.

  • Open SmartLead or Instantly dashboard each evening. Check metrics for each active campaign.
  • Kill immediately if: cost per reply too high, bounce rate above 5%, zero replies after 500 sends, or you've run out of leads for that segment.
  • If out of leads (not killed): go back to your Qualified Leads sheet, pull the next 5,000 rows for that segment, and upload them to the campaign.
  • Paste the campaign stats into Claude and ask: "Analyze this campaign data. Which email performs best? Should I kill this or continue? What changes would you suggest to the copy?"
  • Log all decisions in a Google Sheet: Campaign Name | Start Date | Total Sent | Reply Rate | Decision | Reason. This becomes your learning database for future campaigns.
Free Tools for This Step
SmartLead Dashboard Claude.ai (Analysis) Google Sheets (Log) ChatGPT
The log sheet you build here feeds directly into the "system learns" module — this is your long-term competitive edge.

Step 1 — Automated Lead Scraping

Apify's free tier runs scheduled actors to scrape LinkedIn, Apollo exports, and company directories automatically. Outputs feed directly into a Google Sheet or Airtable as the raw lead database.

Apify (Free) Apollo CSV Export Google Sheets API n8n Trigger

  • Set Apify actor on a weekly cron schedule — runs without your input.
  • n8n (self-hosted free) listens for new rows in the sheet and triggers Step 2 automatically.
  • Free Apify plan: $5/month free compute units — enough for ~10,000 scrapes/month.
🤖

Step 2 — Automated AI Filtering

n8n passes each lead row to the Claude API (or GPT-4o-mini via OpenAI free tier). A sub-agent checks ICP fit using your scoring prompt and writes the verdict + score back to the sheet. Rows below score 7 are auto-archived.

Claude API GPT-4o-mini n8n (Self-Hosted) Google Sheets

  • Claude Haiku API is the cheapest option: ~$0.25 per million tokens — filtering 2L leads costs < $5 total.
  • n8n batches 100 rows per API call to reduce cost. Self-hosted n8n = $0/month on a free Oracle Cloud VM.
✍️

Step 3 — AI Campaign Writer

When enough qualified leads accumulate for a segment, n8n triggers Claude to write the full 3-email sequence with spintax, self-grade it, and rewrite if scores are low. Final copy is stored in a Notion database via the Notion API.

Claude API Notion API (Free) n8n Airtable

  • Self-grading loop: Claude rates its own copy. If <7/10, it rewrites and re-scores automatically. Max 3 iterations.
  • Campaign copy, segment data, and lead count stored in Notion with a "Pending Review" status tag.
💬

Step 4 — Slack Review Bot

A Slack bot (built with n8n + Slack Webhook — both free) pings you with a formatted campaign card at 9 AM. Reply "approve" to launch, or "change: [note]" to trigger a rewrite. The system waits for your reply before proceeding.

Slack (Free) n8n Webhook Slack API (Free) Notion Update

  • Slack free plan supports all incoming webhooks and bot messages needed for this.
  • n8n watches for your Slack reply and routes to "launch" or "rewrite" branch accordingly.
🚀

Step 5 — Auto-Launch via SmartLead API

Once approved in Slack, n8n calls the SmartLead API to build the campaign, upload the first 5,000 verified leads, configure the schedule and follow-up sequence, and start sending — all with zero clicks inside SmartLead. This is fully zero-touch from approval to sending.

SmartLead API n8n HTTP Request Millionverifier (Free Tier) Google Sheets → CSV

  • SmartLead API endpoints: POST /campaign/create, POST /campaign/leads/upload, POST /campaign/start — all accessible on their basic plan.
  • Leads are pre-verified by Millionverifier (free tier: 100 verifications/day) before upload to keep bounce rate under 5%.
  • n8n splits your 40K leads into 5K batches. Each approved campaign gets its next 5K batch auto-uploaded.
  • n8n is self-hosted free on Oracle Cloud Free Tier (2 VMs free forever). Total infra cost: $0/month.
🌙

Step 6 — Nightly Automated Audit (7:50 PM)

A cron job in n8n runs every night at 7:50 PM. It calls the SmartLead API to pull stats for every active campaign, passes them to Claude for analysis, kills campaigns that hit kill criteria, and uploads the next 5K leads to winners — completely automatically.

n8n Cron Job SmartLead API Claude API Slack Notification Google Sheets Log

Kill Criteria (Auto-Stop):

  • Cost per lead too high vs. benchmark
  • Bounce rate exceeds 5%
  • Zero replies after 500 sends
  • Lead list exhausted for segment

Winner Actions (Auto-Continue):

  • Upload next 5K leads automatically
  • Slack notification with performance summary
  • Logs stats to Google Sheet for learning
  • Claude flags winning angles for next campaign

Compound Advantage

The System Learns Over Time

The real moat isn't the tools — it's the data your campaigns generate. Each campaign makes the next one smarter.

📈

Angles That Get Replies

Every campaign reply is logged with the email angle used. Over time, Claude can identify which value propositions, pain points, and hooks resonate with which segments. You start every new campaign with unfair insight.

🎯

Subject Lines That Convert

Open rate data from SmartLead feeds back into a "winning subject lines" library stored in your Google Sheet or Notion. Claude's next campaign prompt is seeded with top performers from your actual data.

💰

Lowest Cost Segments

The nightly audit logs cost-per-reply by segment. After 60 days, you know exactly which industries, geographies, and company sizes produce the cheapest, fastest results. You shift lead volume to winners.

🔄

ICP Gets Sharper

As Claude flags which filtered leads became replies vs. non-replies, your ICP scoring prompt improves. The system progressively qualifies leads more accurately, meaning fewer wasted sends each cycle.

🚫

Deliverability Patterns

Bounce rates, spam flags, and inbox placement data are tracked per domain and per email template. Patterns that damage deliverability get flagged early before they damage your sender score permanently.

⚡

Faster Campaign Spin-Up

By month 3, your campaign prompt library, your winning subject lines database, and your ICP filter mean a new campaign goes from zero to launch-ready in under 4 minutes. The compound advantage accelerates.
